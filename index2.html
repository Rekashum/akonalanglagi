CPU Heat Sink

A CPU heat sink is a passive cooling device typically made of metal with fins that help dissipate heat generated by the central processing unit (CPU). It's usually paired with a fan to enhance heat dissipation and prevent the CPU from overheating.

Hard-Drive

A hard drive is a non-volatile storage device used to store and retrieve digital data. It consists of one or more rigid disks coated with a magnetic material and housed inside a metal or plastic enclosure. Hard drives are commonly used for long-term storage of operating systems, applications, and user files.

Optical Drive

An optical drive is a device used for reading and writing data from optical discs such as CDs, DVDs, and Blu-ray discs. It uses a laser beam to read and write data stored on the discs.

Ram Modules

RAM (Random Access Memory) modules are volatile memory modules used by computers to temporarily store data that the CPU needs to access quickly. RAM modules come in various capacities and speeds and are installed on the motherboard.

CPU-Processor

The CPU (Central Processing Unit) is the primary component of a computer responsible for executing instructions and performing calculations. It is often referred to as the brain of the computer and is responsible for executing programs and tasks.

Motherboard

The motherboard is the main circuit board of a computer system. It provides the electrical connections between the other components of the system, such as the CPU, RAM, hard drives, and expansion cards. The motherboard also houses essential components such as the BIOS, chipset, and connectors for peripherals.

SMPS Power Supply

The SMPS (Switched-Mode Power Supply) is a type of power supply that converts AC power from the electrical outlet into DC power required by the computer's components. It regulates voltage and current to ensure stable and reliable power delivery to the system.

System Cooling Fan

System cooling fans are active cooling devices used to dissipate heat generated by various components within the computer system, such as the CPU, GPU, and power supply. They help maintain optimal operating temperatures and prevent overheating, which can damage components and degrade performance.
